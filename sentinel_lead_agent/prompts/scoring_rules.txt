CMMC 2.0 BUYER LEAD SCORING RULES

Score UP — strong buyer-side signals
+3  Active DoD prime or subcontract confirmed (SAM.gov, CAGE code, federal award evidence)
+2  DFARS 252.204-7012 or DFARS 252.204-7021 clause language on website or in evidence
+2  ITAR, EAR, or export-controlled product lines — company handles controlled goods
+2  Explicit CMMC language: "CMMC 2.0", "Level 2 certification", "CMMC in progress"
+2  Compliance signals: NIST 800-171, SSP, POA&M, CUI handling mentioned
+1  Defense manufacturing, aerospace, engineering, or defense R&D delivery
+1  Located in primary target region (MD, VA, DC)
+1  SMB size (10–500 employees) — limited internal compliance capacity likely
+1  Compliance pain points visible (no GRC tool named, rapid DoD growth, small IT team)

Score DOWN — weak or disqualifying signals
-2  No sign of government, defense, or regulated delivery work in any evidence
-2  Large enterprise (>1,000 employees) with likely internal compliance capacity
-1  Located outside the target region with no offsetting signals
-1  Mature security governance or compliance program already advertised

HARD REJECT — score 1–2, label as competitor or non-buyer
  C3PAO, RPO, CMMC consultant, gap assessor, readiness accelerator
  Compliance-as-a-service, managed compliance, virtual CISO, vCISO
  MSSP or MSP whose primary offering is compliance delivery or security services
  Marketing language: "we help you achieve CMMC", "CMMC services", "your CMMC partner"
  University, government agency, nonprofit

SCORE BANDS
  9–10: Immediate priority buyer — multiple strong DoD signals, clear CMMC pressure
   7–8: Strong fit — solid evidence of defense delivery and likely CMMC obligation
   4–6: Moderate fit — some defense or compliance signals but evidence is incomplete
   1–3: Weak fit or disqualified — provider profile, no defense signal, or hard reject
